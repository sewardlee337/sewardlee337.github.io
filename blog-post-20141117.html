A Taiwan Public Data Directory

November 17, 2014

Looking for Taiwan data can be an incredibly frustrating ordeal.

If you're a foreign scholar who (a) cannot read Traditional Chinese, or (b) do not have preexisting familiarity with Taiwan's government structure, the search can be as fun as eating broken glass. Part of the problem is that international organizations such as the World Bank and the OECD carry limited, if any, Taiwan data. The fact that Taiwan's government has largely missed the open data bandwagon only exacerbates the problem (CommonWealth Magazine has done a great job at profiling this issue).

In any event, below is my tiny contribution towards addressing this problem. To help analysts find what they need, I have compiled and categorized sources of Taiwan data that I am currently aware of, and turned the list into an interactive directory powered by Tableau (an idea I stole from the Tableau Public website).

To further reduce the amount of time that analysts have to spend staring at garbage data, I have scored each data source for general usefulness on a scale of 1 to 5. This score is a simple average of individual scores that I assigned under the following six dimensions:

  1. What file format is it offered in? Excel and CSV files are awesome. Data embedded in a webpage or offered in a PDF file are a pain in the ass to work with.
  2. Is it historical? You can't see changes over time if your data doesn't stretch far enough into the past.
  3. Is it voluminous? The more data, the better. Pretty self-explanatory.
  4. Is it multivariate? A 360-degree view of the phenomenon can only be generated if you have data for all the relevant variables.
  5. Is it granular? You can't slice and dice the data if it's not offered with enough detail.
  6. Is it clear and user-friendly? Data offered exclusively in Traditional Chinese received low scores, because relatively few people outside of Taiwan would understand it. Data only offered in Minguo calendar years (Gregorian year minus 1911) received low scores for the same reason.

I'm a strong believer in open data - I believe in Sergey Brin's call to make information "universally accessible and useful." So I eagerly await the day when someone makes my work here obsolete. In the meantime, feel free to shoot me an email at sewardlee337@gmail.com if you have suggestions for improvement.
